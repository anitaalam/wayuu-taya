About the Foundation

Since 2002, we have worked to improve the living quality of indigenous communities across Latin America — always respecting their traditions, cultures, and beliefs.

Our Mission

To help improve the lives of Latin-

American indigenous people while

maintaining and respecting their

traditions, culture and beliefs.

We bring food, medicine, education, and

clean water to the most vulnerable

communities along the border between

Venezuela and Colombia.

Our Vision Hover to explore ↗

Access to food, health care, water and livelihoods for the most vulnerable communities in the indigenous region along the northern Venezuela-Colombia border.

Our Objectives Hover to explore ↗
  • Create community centers integrating health, nutrition, and education services
  • Preserve the cultural heritage of Wayuu women, respecting their traditions and values
  • Provide sustainable agriculture through agroecology programs
  • Deliver clean water to communities in the La Guajira desert
How It Began

A call from La Guajira

In 2002, Patricia Velasquez — Wayuu model, actress, and activist — received a call from her cousin about the village on the border between Venezuela and Colombia where her mother was born. One child a day was dying, mostly from malnutrition.

She gathered friends to think of a way to address the problem. With their first donation, they were able to install a water pump in a Wayuu village. That's how the foundation started.

Child welfare was the chief concern, so the foundation unified the goals of fighting famine and educational deficiency by setting up schools where children could learn and receive two meals a day.

Our Journey

Over Two Decades of Change

2002

Foundation Established

Patricia Velasquez founds the Wayuu Taya Foundation to support indigenous communities in La Guajira.

2003

UNESCO Recognition

Patricia Velasquez appointed UNESCO Artist for Peace for her humanitarian work.

2010s

Expanding Reach

Programs expanded to include hundreds of thousands of families across multiple regions of Venezuela.

2020

Pandemic Response

Delivered thousands of PPE kits, medications, hand sanitizer, and soap to over 40,000 people.

2022

Water for All Launched

Established the "Water for Community" initiative, delivering clean water to remote desert communities.

Today

One Million Lives

Over one million people reached across 85 communities, with ongoing programs in food, health, education, water, and music.
