Help for Those in Need

Bringing food, medicine, education, and essential services to the most vulnerable communities along the Venezuela-Colombia border.

Our Impact

22 Years of Uninterrupted Work

In 2024, we celebrated 22 years of uninterrupted work, contributing to improving the quality of life for over one million people in Venezuela. During 2023 alone, our permanent health, food, education, agriculture, and water programs benefited nearly 229,000 people.

The Wayuu Taya Foundation strives to make life better by bringing food, access to medicine, and access to education to the most vulnerable, in localities along the border between Venezuela and Colombia.

What We Do

Focus Areas

Food & Nutrition

Thanks to Acceso, since the program started in 2019 and as of December 31, 2023, we managed 215 loads, distributing 990,828 kg of food and providing 3,256,223 meals, which benefited 75,829 people.

189,159 kg Food distributed in 2023
626,299 Meals served in 2023
15,887 People benefited in 2023

As of September 2024, we distributed 34 loads, totaling 118,112 kg of food, served 383,937 meals, benefiting 13,299 people, including 12,238 children across 49 schools.

Health & Medicine

It is essential for the Wayuu Taya Foundation to lend its unconditional support to indigenous communities in regards to health and nutrition, especially since these communities tend to face extreme poverty and malnutrition.

142,583 People received medicine (2020–2023 via Direct Relief)
55,157 People received medicine in 2023 alone
24,712 People received medicine (Jan–Sep 2024)

The Foundation supports vaccination campaigns for children and teenagers, and provides medical equipment and medication to health institutions in areas adjacent to the Wayúu community.

Hygiene & Prevention

We made progress by installing handwashing stations in areas where we raised awareness about their use and importance in preventing COVID-19 and other diseases, benefiting 6,840 people.

109,126 People received hygiene items (Dec 2022–Dec 2023, Colgate Palmolive)
62,427 People from 252 institutions (Jan–Sep 2024, Colgate Palmolive)

Eye Care

In partnership with VSP Vision Care, our ophthalmology health drives bring essential eye care services directly to communities that otherwise have no access to vision specialists.

1,070 Children benefited in 2023
